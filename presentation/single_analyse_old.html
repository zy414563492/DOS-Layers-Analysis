Dilation & Erosion¶

By dilation and erosion, we can remove discrete points and make the image form a connected domain as much as possible.

This operation is only used for ellipse fitting since it improves the ellipse fitting performance a lot, and the distortion of some data does not affect the correctness of the ellipse fitting result too much. However, in Gaussian fitting, we need to ensure the integrity of the data to get the right results.

Gaussian fitting(xy-axes)¶

Theory¶

The model of the two-dimensional Gaussian function is expressed as:

$$ f(x,y)=Aexp(-\frac{(x-x_0)^2}{2\sigma_x^2}-\frac{(y-y_0)^2}{2\sigma_y^2}) $$

In order to facilitate the calculation, take the logarithm on both sides and get:

$$ lnf=lnA-\frac{(x-x_0)^2}{2\sigma_x^2}-\frac{(y-y_0)^2}{2\sigma_y^2} $$

Expand and further transform into: $$ lnf=ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e $$

$$ \begin{Bmatrix} a=-\frac{1}{2\sigma_x^2} \\ b=-\frac{1}{2\sigma_y^2} \\ c=\frac{x_0}{\sigma_x^2} \\ d=\frac{y_0}{\sigma_y^2} \\ e= lnA-\frac{x_0}{2\sigma_x^2}-\frac{y_0}{2\sigma_y^2} \end{Bmatrix} $$

Then it becomes a binary quadratic polynomial fitting problem.

Least squares solution to minimize the sum of squared residuals:

$$ Q=min\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lny)^2 $$

Find the partial derivative for each variable and make it zero, and get the equations:

$$ \begin{Bmatrix} 2\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lnf)\Sigma x^2=0 \\ 2\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lnf)\Sigma y^2=0 \\ 2\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lnf)\Sigma x=0 \\ 2\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lnf)\Sigma y=0 \\ 2\Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e-lnf)\Sigma 1=0 \end{Bmatrix} $$

Further transformation:

$$ \begin{Bmatrix} \Sigma(ax^4+by^2x^2+cx^3+dx^2y+ex^2)=\Sigma x^2lnf \\ \Sigma(ax^2y^2+by^4+cxy^2+dy^3+ey^2)=\Sigma y^2lnf \\ \Sigma(ax^3+bxy^2+cx^2+dxy+ex)=\Sigma xlnf \\ \Sigma(ax^2y+by^3+cxy+dy^2+ey)=\Sigma ylnf \\ \Sigma(ax^2+by^2+cx+dy+e)=\Sigma lnf \end{Bmatrix} $$

Get the linear equations:

$$ BK=C $$

in which,

$$ B =\begin{bmatrix} \Sigma x^4 & \Sigma x^2y^2 & \Sigma x^3 & \Sigma x^2y & \Sigma x^2 \\ \Sigma x^2y^2 & \Sigma y^4 & \Sigma xy^2 & \Sigma y^3 & \Sigma y^2 \\ \Sigma x^3 & \Sigma xy^2 & \Sigma x^2 & \Sigma xy & \Sigma x \\ \Sigma x^2y & \Sigma y^3 & \Sigma xy & \Sigma y^2 & \Sigma y \\ \Sigma x^2 & \Sigma y^2 & \Sigma x & \Sigma y & \Sigma 1 \end{bmatrix} $$$$ K=\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \\ d \\ e \end{bmatrix} $$$$ C=\begin{bmatrix} \Sigma x^2lnf \\ \Sigma y^2lnf \\ \Sigma xlnf \\ \Sigma ylnf \\ \Sigma lnf \end{bmatrix} $$

Solving this linear equations to get the parameters of Gaussian function:

$$ K=B^{-1}C $$

def Gaussian_fit(x, y, value):
    C = np.array([np.sum(x ** 2 * np.log(value)),
                  np.sum(y ** 2 * np.log(value)),
                  np.sum(x * np.log(value)),
                  np.sum(y * np.log(value)),
                  np.sum(np.log(value))])

    B = np.array([[np.sum(x ** 4), np.sum(x ** 2 * y ** 2), np.sum(x ** 3), np.sum(x ** 2 * y), np.sum(x ** 2)],
                  [np.sum(x ** 2 * y ** 2), np.sum(y ** 4), np.sum(x * y ** 2), np.sum(y ** 3), np.sum(y ** 2)],
                  [np.sum(x ** 3), np.sum(x * y ** 2), np.sum(x ** 2), np.sum(x * y), np.sum(x)],
                  [np.sum(x ** 2 * y), np.sum(y ** 3), np.sum(x * y), np.sum(y ** 2), np.sum(y)],
                  [np.sum(x ** 2), np.sum(y ** 2), np.sum(x), np.sum(y), len(value)]])

    B_inv = np.linalg.inv(B)
    K = B_inv.dot(C.T)
    return K

Gaussian fitting(rotation)¶

Theory¶

$$ f(x,y)=Aexp(-\frac{(x-x_0)^2}{2\sigma_x^2}-\frac{(y-y_0)^2}{2\sigma_y^2}) $$$$ \begin{bmatrix}x' \\ y'\end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} cos\theta & -sin\theta \\ sin\theta & cos\theta \end{bmatrix}* \begin{bmatrix}x \\ y\end{bmatrix} $$$$ \theta\in(0,\frac{\pi}{2}) $$$$ argmin(\sigma_x^2+\sigma_y^2) $$

Implementation¶

In [32]:
def Gaussian_fit_rotation(x, y, value):
    best_score = 999999999
    best_angle = None
    for i in range(0, 100):
        # Generate random angle values from 0 to 90
        angle = random.uniform(0, 90)
        radian = angle * np.pi / 180

        x_new = np.array(x) * np.cos(radian) - np.array(y) * np.sin(radian)
        y_new = np.array(x) * np.sin(radian) + np.array(y) * np.cos(radian)
        M = Gaussian_fit(x_new, y_new, np.array(value))

        # find the least cost
        current_score = 1 / (-2 * M[0]) + 1 / (-2 * M[1])
        # cost = costf(x_A, y_A, value_A, angle * np.pi / 180)

        if current_score < best_score:
            best_score = current_score
            best_angle = angle
            K = M

    K = np.append(K, best_angle)
    return K

def luminanace(r, g, b):
    a = np.array(list(map(lambda x: x / 12.92 if x <= 0.03928 else pow((x + 0.055) / 1.055, 2.4), [r, g, b])))
    c = np.array([0.2126, 0.7152, 0.0722])
    return np.sum(a * c)


def contrast(rgb1, rgb2):
    return (luminanace(rgb1[0], rgb1[1], rgb1[2]) + 0.05) / (luminanace(rgb2[0], rgb2[1], rgb2[2]) + 0.05)
